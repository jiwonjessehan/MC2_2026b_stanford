\section{Scientific Justification}

The Large Magellanic Cloud is the nearest star-forming disk galaxy to the Milky Way, providing an external vantage point on resolved stellar populations, compact-object binaries, and the multiphase interstellar medium at $\sim1\,\mathrm{pc}$ scales that no Galactic survey can access. Yet despite deep imaging (SMASH, \citealt{nidever2017}) and Gaia astrometry, \emph{spectroscopic} coverage of the LMC disk has been limited to the brightest OB stars and Wolf-Rayets (e.g.\ VLT-FLAMES, \citealt{vlt_flames}, $\sim900$ stars at $V<17$), leaving the main-sequence turn-off (MSTO) and most of the ISM unexplored. Multi-epoch, contiguous spectroscopy at MSTO depth is the decisive step for two science drivers: (i)~discovering and characterizing compact-object binaries --- the channel through which VFTS~243 \citep{shenar2022} and the hypervelocity-star tracers of a putative $10^6\,\ms$ LMC black hole \citep{han2025} were uncovered, and (ii)~resolving the warm ionized ISM on sub-parsec scales that connect to cloud-scale physics.

\noindent\textsc{\textsf{(MC)$^2$}} --- The Magellanic Clouds Magellan Campaign --- is a contiguous LLAMAS IFU survey of the central LMC designed to deliver both. LLAMAS's combination of wide spectral coverage (3500--9500\,\AA), $R\sim2000$, IFU format, and Magellan's aperture yields $\sim5\,\kms$ RV precision on MSTO stars ($r\sim20$) in $10\,\mathrm{min}$ per epoch --- $\sim20\times$ more stars per arcmin$^2$ than VLT-FLAMES at the same cadence. The same data cubes simultaneously produce continuum-subtracted, velocity-resolved emission-line maps that no narrowband imaging survey can match (\S Demo~3 below).

\noindent In 2025B--2026A the team carried out a pilot of 8 nights covering a $\sim3'\times3'$ region centered on the LMC kinematic center at (RA, Dec) $=(80.90^\circ, -68.74^\circ)$ \citep{wan2020}. \textbf{The full program requires $\sim10$ epochs per field across the $6'\times6'$ footprint} --- 10 is the minimum cadence for folding short-period binaries and phase-resolving RV curves \citep[cf.][]{vlt_flames}, and also the integration depth needed to reach the [S\,{\sc ii}]/H$\alpha$ ratio in diffuse inter-filament gas. At our observing efficiency the full program is a $\sim 18$-night, multi-year campaign; the present 2026B request is one step in that multi-year plan. Pipelines and analyses are now mature: below we present \textbf{four concrete demonstrations from pilot data in hand} that motivate the 2026B continuation.

\noindent\textbf{1.~Confident re-detection of VFTS~243 validates absolute RV precision (Fig.~\ref{fig:vfts}).} Our footprint contains VFTS~243, the O-star + $\ge 9\,\ms$ black-hole binary of \citet{shenar2022}, in a 10.4-day orbit. Across \textbf{8 independent epochs} spanning 5 months we recover the published orbit blind (without fitting any orbital parameters) at $\chi^2=5.8$ (8 d.o.f.) using He\,{\sc ii}\,5411. The scatter about the Shenar et al.\ ephemeris is $\sim5\,\kms$, and is consistent across benches, channels, and nights---demonstrating $\lesssim5\,\kms$ absolute RV zero-point stability after our per-fiber barycentric and sky-line wavelength calibration. This is the precision required to detect and characterize new compact-object binaries with \textsc{\textsf{(MC)$^2$}}.

\noindent\textbf{2.~A population of RV variable candidates in hand, more epochs needed.} A cross-correlation analysis against a self-template identifies \textbf{tens} of RV-variable candidates across the pilot footprint, including main-sequence turn-off stars as faint as $r\sim19.6$ (example in Fig.~\ref{fig:msp}). Because VFTS~243 was prioritized in the pilot, most candidates currently have only 2--4 epochs---enough to flag variability but insufficient to fold orbits or reject systematics. \textbf{The 2026B epochs are the decisive step}: they raise the epoch count by $\sim50\%$ across the field, enabling robust period searches and orbital solutions for the candidate sample. SMASH photometry \citep{nidever2017} predicts $\gtrsim$18{,}000 usable stars in the full (MC)$^2$ footprint, compared with $\sim$900 stars monitored by VLT-FLAMES \citep{vlt_flames}.

\noindent\textbf{3.~Sub-parsec maps of the warm ionized ISM (Fig.~\ref{fig:ism}).} IFU spectroscopy allows continuum-subtracted, velocity-resolved emission-line maps that no narrowband imaging survey can produce. Figure~\ref{fig:ism} shows our H$\alpha$ map \textbf{side-by-side with DeMCELS} \citep{points2024}, the current state-of-the-art wide-field LMC H$\alpha$ map (DECam 4\,m, PSF $\sim1''$). Over the same field (MC)$^2$ resolves filamentary structure, bubble rims, and sharp ionization fronts that DeMCELS smooths away. In addition, we produce [O\,{\sc iii}]\,5007, [N\,{\sc ii}]\,6548/84, and [S\,{\sc ii}]\,6717/31 maps at matching resolution, together with a per-spaxel H$\alpha$ Gaussian centroid kinematic map with $\sim5\,\kms$ precision---an entirely new capability for this region of the LMC and the foundation for quantitative studies of ISM mixing and structure (led by Kado-Fong, Nowotka, and Clark).

\noindent\textbf{4.~Joint stellar-parameter + multi-epoch RV fitting with \texttt{MINESweeper} (Fig.~\ref{fig:msp}).} We have implemented a multi-epoch joint fitter built on \texttt{MINESweeper} \citep{cargile2020}: each star's $\log g$, $T_{\rm eff}$, $[\mathrm{Fe}/\mathrm{H}]$, and $[\alpha/\mathrm{Fe}]$ are held fixed across epochs while a per-epoch RV is fit jointly with broadband photometry and the LLAMAS spectrum. Leveraging the full stellar library and the shared SED boosts faint-star RV precision below what a blind single-epoch CCF can achieve. Figure~\ref{fig:msp} shows a test fit on a $r=19.6$ main-sequence turn-off candidate from the pilot. Demonstration 4 is the engine we will use to confirm the candidates of Demonstration 2.

\section{Stanford-led project}

\textsc{\textsf{(MC)$^2$}} is a Stanford-led effort. Stanford postdoc \textbf{Jesse Han} conceived the campaign, designed its observing strategy, and has executed essentially all of the analysis shown above (VFTS~243 recovery, CCF variable search, H$\alpha$/[O\,{\sc iii}]/[N\,{\sc ii}]/[S\,{\sc ii}] maps, Gaussian kinematics, \texttt{MINESweeper} multi-epoch fitting). Stanford PhD student \textbf{Viraj Manwadkar} developed the improved LLAMAS reduction pipeline---specifically the MISTY sky subtraction and per-bench flux calibration---whose impact is visible in Figure~\ref{fig:reduction}. These corrections are what make accurate absolute RVs and continuum-subtracted line maps possible. The remaining Stanford collaborators (Kado-Fong, Nowotka, Clark) lead the ISM analysis strand.

\section{Observing plan and time request}

\textbf{(MC)$^2$ is a multi-year program.} Reaching \textbf{10 epochs per field across $6'\times6'$} is required both for orbital fits of the RV-variable candidates from Demo~2 and for reaching the [S\,{\sc ii}]/H$\alpha$ depth needed for per-spaxel ISM diagnostics. One full-field epoch is $\sim24$ exposure-hours (10\,min per pointing with a $30''$ step and $>4''$ overlap for seamless tiling); the full program is thus $\sim18$ nights, to be accumulated over $\sim2$ additional cycles. The pilot (8 nights, 2025B--2026A) delivered the demonstrations above on a $3'\times3'$ sub-field; the present \textbf{3-night 2026B request (2 dark, 1 gray)} is the next natural step.

These 3 nights will achieve three concrete goals. \textbf{(a)} \emph{Confirm the Demonstration-2 RV variable candidates} by filling in their light curves to 5--7 epochs --- enough to phase-fold candidate orbits on the way to the 10-epoch program goal. \textbf{(b)} \emph{Tile contiguously to the full $6'\times6'$ region} centered on the LMC kinematic center, covering the $1\sigma$ kinematic-center uncertainty \citep{wan2020} --- the minimum area needed to fit a rotation model that can localize the SMBH candidate \citep{han2025} to within a few arcseconds. \textbf{(c)} \emph{Deepen ISM maps} toward the final [S\,{\sc ii}]/H$\alpha$ diagnostic S/N in the diffuse inter-filament medium. Follow-on time in 2027A--2027B will complete the 10-epoch survey.

The LLAMAS ETC predicts S/N$\sim5$ per pixel on an $r=21$ K-star in a 10-min exposure; the MINESweeper joint fit of Demo~4 drives per-star precision well below this single-epoch floor when combined across epochs. Earlier LLAMAS multi-epoch RV work by Chickles \& Burdge (AM\,CVn He\,{\sc ii} campaign) with an identical setup corroborates the feasibility of this cadence.
